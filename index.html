How is a college's score put together?

The plain-language version — the same numbers you see in any college's breakdown.

Think of the score as a report card with six subjects. Every college gets marks out of 100 in each subject. The subjects aren't equally important, so each one contributes a fixed slice of the final score. Add up the slices and you get the Core Score (0–100).

💰 Graduate salaries — 25% of the score

How well this college's graduates are paid, compared to the best-paying college in the list. The top college scores 100; a college paying half as much scores 50.

🏅 National ranking (NIRF) — 16.7%

The college's position in the government's national ranking over the last three years (this year counts most). A better rank means more marks; unranked colleges get very few.

📜 Quality grade (NAAC) — 16.7%

The national quality-accreditation grade, converted to marks: A++ is 100, A+ is 90, and so on down to 0 for no grade.

🎓 Programme accreditation (NBA) — 16.7%

How much of the college's teaching is independently accredited, relative to the best college in the list.

⭐ Brand perception — 12.5%

A reputation estimate combining how much employers want its graduates, the strength of its alumni network, placement outcomes, student quality and public visibility.

🎯 Our own priority tag (CG) — 12.5%

The hiring team's own call: P1 (top priority) counts as 100 marks, P2 as 50, no tag as 0.

Then two optional bonuses go on top (the switches at the top of the page):

🤝 Engagement bonus — colleges where we currently do little engagement get the biggest boost (+10), because that's where extra effort pays off most. Medium engagement gets +5, already-high engagement +2.
🌈 Diversity bonus — +5 for campuses with a strong diversity profile.

💼 Proposed salary-grade ladder (reference only — does not affect the score)

A3 ₹3.25L · A4 ₹4.25L · A4_Premium ₹5.75L · A5 ₹7.5L · B1 ₹10L + ₹1L joining bonus · B2 ₹12L + ₹1.5L · C1 ₹15L + ₹1.5L · C1_Premium ₹18L + ₹2L. Each college is tagged with the grade nearest to its median salary — e.g. a ₹7.5L median reads as grade A5, a ₹12L median as B2 — so salary conversations can happen in grade language.

Core Score + bonuses = Total Priority Score. "Overall Rank" simply orders every college by that total. The main list additionally keeps our P1 colleges first, then P2, then the rest — so the business's priorities always lead the page.

Every college is marked out of 100 in six areas, and each area contributes a fixed slice of the Core Score: graduate salaries 25%, national ranking 16.7%, quality grade 16.7%, programme accreditation 16.7%, brand perception 12.5% and our own CG priority tag 12.5%. Optional bonus points for engagement opportunity and diversity are added on top when toggled, giving the Total Priority Score. “Rank” is the list position (P1 colleges first, then P2, then the rest, by total score); “Overall Rank” orders every college purely by total score.
